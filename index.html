The current state of identity

Individuals and corporations are not in control of their digital identities. Sensitive personal data is stored in a centralized source and is shared without the consent of its owners. Furthermore, all digital personas undergo intense scrutiny, precisely due to the ease of faking a digital persona compared to a physical identity. Recent changes in compliance and regulations bring the need for change in how identity should be managed.
CREDPROOF aims to tackle the identity issue by using a decentralized approach to create a platform to issue verifiable credentials, which are privacy enhanced and self-sovereign by design.

Characteristics

The CREDPROOF platform focuses on the incorporation of the following characteristics by design.

Trusted and
Trustworthy

Founded on an existing real-world identity which has a high degree of source of trust, where its attributes have a high degree of provenance. The passports are issued by trusted third party operating within a legal jurisdiction (e.g. Bank, Government, Insurance Company.)

Interoperable and
Useful Everywhere

There will be standardized and reliable credentials in widespread use in both the public and private sectors. If a individuals presents a valid and appropriate credential, any qualified inspector is capable of accepting and verifying the credential as proof of identity and attributes.

Privacy Respected
(GDPR Compliant)

All data, attestations, validation and transactions are cryptographically secured and every transmission and transaction is user consent based. Legitimacy of mailto:information and transactions for all parties – quicker and more reliable for everyone. GDPR compliant by design (No PII).

Our Approach

The CREDPROOF platform by design can adapt to various use cases, such as insurance, health, finance and education.

1. Examine (By Issuer)

Perform required vetting, due diligence, and other tasks needed to establish confidence in making an attestation about an identity/credential trait.

2. Issue (By Issuer)

Generate and deliver a digital credential in accordance with some predefined schema.

3. Hold
(By Holder / Subject)

The individual or entity in control of the digital passport or agent that stores and controls the use of a given Credential; note, the Holder may or may not be the Subject (e.g. a child may be the Subject of a passport, but the child’s parent may be the Holder of that passport).

4. Use (By Holder)

Holder presents one or more credentials to a inspector.

5. Verify (By Inspector)

Validate authenticity of issuer and holder and then consume data.

Background Checks

Issuer
Holder
Verifier

1. Examine

An institution performs a process to determine if a credential is earned

2. Issue

The institution generate and delivers a digital credential in accordance with predefined schema

3. Hold

A prospective worker is being background checked by a potential employer who request certain and specific credentials from relevant institution form the candidate.

4. Use

The candidate presents zero-knowledge proofs of requested attributes drawn from verifiable digital credentials issued by the institution.

5. Verify

The prospective employer validates the authenticity of the institution and the candidate inspects the data (zero-knowledge proofs of credential attributes), and decides to hire the candidate(or not).
